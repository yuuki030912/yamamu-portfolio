Palworld 1.0 Beginner's Guide: Best Settings, Fast Leveling, and the Base-Building Trap Everyone Falls Into

By Yamamu Updated July 2026 8 min read 日本語版はこちら

TL;DR: change your world settings first, craft stone tools immediately, catch Pals in bulk instead of fighting them, and never build your base on a single foundation. Palworld has officially hit full release — and the opening hours decide whether the game feels like a grind or an adventure.

1. Change These World Settings Before You Start

Palworld lets you customize your world before creating it, and the default settings are tuned for slow, survival-style progression. If you want to enjoy the game rather than grind through it, adjust three settings:

SettingRecommended valueWhy
EXP Rate20xSkip the grind — reach the fun mid-game content in your first session
Work Speed5xCrafting and building finish in seconds instead of minutes
Building Deterioration0 (off)Your structures never decay while you're away
💡 Purists can play on defaults, but these settings remove friction without removing challenge. You can change most world settings later, so nothing here locks you in.

2. Your First Hour: Craft in This Order

Once you're in the world, resist the urge to wander. The early game has a clear "correct" order:

  1. Primitive Workbench — unlocks everything else
  2. Stone Axe — wood gathering speeds up dramatically
  3. Stone Pickaxe — stone and ore for your first real structures

Use fast travel points (the giant glowing statues) as soon as you find them, and follow the on-screen missions — they're effectively a tutorial that pays you in XP and resources.

3. The Fast-Leveling Secret: Catch Everything, Fight Nothing

This is the single biggest beginner tip in the video: catching Pals gives far more XP than defeating them — and catching the same species repeatedly stacks a capture bonus.

Instead of fighting your way through the starting plains, craft a stack of Pal Spheres and mass-catch every Lamball, Cattiva, and Chikipi you see. With the 20x EXP setting from step 1, you'll gain multiple levels in minutes. It front-loads your stat points and technology unlocks.

Where to spend those unlocks: prioritize technology that expands your base (Palbox, storage, beds, feeding) over weapons. Your Pals will do the fighting and the working — your job is infrastructure.

4. Base Building: Let Your Pals Do the Work

Place your Palbox early — it defines your base area and lets assigned Pals work autonomously. Once Pals are stationed at your base, they will chop wood, mine stone, transport materials, craft items, water crops, generate power, and cook — depending on species.

Every Pal has work suitabilities (kindling, watering, handiwork, transporting, mining, and so on). Check them before assigning: a Cattiva hauls materials, a Foxparks lights your furnace. Matching Pals to jobs is the difference between a base that runs itself and a base full of idle Pals. Not sure who to catch first? See our 10 best early-game Pals.

Two survival notes for your first nights:

  • Night raids happen. Build basic defenses (or at least walls) before dark.
  • Hunger is constant. Set up a feed box and a berry plantation early so both you and your Pals stay fed while you explore.

5. The Building Trap That Destroys Entire Bases

Here's the mistake the video warns about most strongly: in Palworld, structures depend on their foundations. If a foundation block is destroyed, everything built on top of it collapses with it.

New players love building tall — a whole base stacked on a few foundation tiles. Then a raid (or a stray attack from your own Pal) breaks one foundation block, and the entire structure chain-collapses.

The fix is simple:

  • Spread your base across multiple independent foundations rather than one connected platform
  • Keep critical stations (Palbox, storage, beds) on separate foundation groups
  • With Building Deterioration set to 0 (step 1), you eliminate decay-related collapses entirely

Quick-Start Checklist

  • ☑ Set EXP 20x / Work Speed 5x / Deterioration 0 before creating your world
  • ☑ Craft: Workbench → Stone Axe → Stone Pickaxe
  • ☑ Unlock fast travel statues as you find them
  • ☑ Mass-catch early Pals for stacked capture XP
  • ☑ Place your Palbox and assign Pals by work suitability
  • ☑ Build a feed box + berry farm before night one
  • ☑ Split your base across multiple foundations

FAQ

Is Palworld worth starting at full release?

Yes — full release is the best entry point the game has had. The early game is significantly smoother than early access, especially with adjusted world settings.

Do the recommended settings disable achievements or multiplayer?

No. World settings are a built-in feature, not a mod. You can also revisit most of them after world creation.

What's the fastest way to level up in early Palworld?

Catching Pals — repeatedly. Capture XP with the species bonus far outpaces combat XP, especially at boosted EXP rates.

Why did my whole base just collapse?

Almost certainly a destroyed foundation block. Structures inherit stability from what's under them — rebuild across multiple independent foundation groups.
